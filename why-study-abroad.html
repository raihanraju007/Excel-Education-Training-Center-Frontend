Rationale

Why Studying Abroad Matters

In a globalized, well-connected world, employers increasingly value graduates with international experience and education. Studying abroad helps you to learn new languages, appreciate other cultures, overcome challenges of living in another country and gain a greater understanding of the world.

Nothing beats the immersive international experience you get while studying abroad — from new outlooks and customs to the chance to travel across neighboring countries.

Benefits & Challenges

What You Gain

See the World

Experience new terrains, museums and landmarks of your host nation — and travel to neighboring countries too.

Different Education

Discover sides of your major you may not have seen at home and immerse in a new education system.

Master a Language

Total immersion is the best way to learn — combined with formal courses at your host university.

New Culture

Go beyond a purely academic experience by deeply understanding people, traditions and culture.

Independence

Studying abroad really brings out your independent nature and makes you an explorer of your new nation.

Self-Discovery

Tests your ability to adapt and problem solve — a benefit that lasts a lifetime.

Preparation

Your Step-by-Step Roadmap

Step 1

Choose Your Destination

Think about tuition costs, living costs, graduate career prospects and overall safety. Match destination to your lifestyle.

Step 2

Pick a Program & University

Look closely at courses offered, local lifestyle, admission requirements and costs. Use world university rankings.

Step 3

Prepare Your Application

Some countries require a two-step application — one for the university, one for the program. Submit on the official site.

Step 4

Take Required Tests

Most programs need IELTS / TOEFL / GRE / GMAT. We offer IELTS and pre-IELTS preparation at EETC.

Step 5

Apply for Visa

After receiving the letter of acceptance, apply for your student visa. Allow several months — we handle the entire process.

Skills Development

Learn Soft Skills for Your Application

Universities want students who are affluent in computing, paper-writing and communicating. Find out what your university is looking for and aim to master one skill every semester.

  • Plan your courses — choose ones that bring diversity to your major.
  • Study for standardized testing — GRE, GMAT, IELTS or TOEFL.
  • Foster relationships with faculty members for strong recommendations.
  • Build your own research or independent study project.
Funding

Managing Money & Scholarships

From scholarships to fellowships, studentships, sponsorships, grants and bursaries — many funding opportunities make studying abroad more affordable.

Merit Scholarships

Granted based on academic merit — highly competitive but rewarding.

Funding Schemes

Targeted schemes for students from developing countries and specific subjects.

Government Grants

Research governmental schemes in your home and study country.

External Partners

Funding from external organizations relating to your field of study.

Pre-Assessment Session

Plan from Your Very First Semester

For students determined to receive a master's degree from a highly-ranked university abroad, the best time to prepare is as soon as you can. Here are a few things to do to jump start your application 4 years from your 1st semester.

  • Schedule certain courses ahead of testing deadlines.
  • Find out transcript requirements of different universities.
  • Begin English vocabulary preparation early — Word Smart by Princeton Review.
  • Solve past IELTS papers to map your strengths and weaknesses.
