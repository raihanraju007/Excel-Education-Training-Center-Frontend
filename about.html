Background

Our Journey Since 2015

Excel Education & Training Center (EETC) started its journey in 2015 as a business to improve lives through academic excellence. From the very beginning, we hired IT professionals and trained them focusing on international market demand and supported them for international work placement crossing national borders.

In 2017, EETC inaugurated student visa support for both English speaking and non-English speaking countries and started processing student visas in Japan and the UK.

EETC has offered basic and advance Japanese Language courses, IELTS and pre-IELTS courses — pre-requisites for higher study — and has assisted students in getting visas to study abroad.

Message from Management

"Together We Achieve MORE"

We envision that only quality education can improve the family and the nation. The young educated students are the backbone of a nation, and many of them are experiencing high scarcity of quality higher education. Sending proper students to proper foreign universities benefits our nation.

EETC delivers a truly global experience and helps you to think globally and compete strategically in any economy. We strongly contemplate that education entrepreneurship is not only a business — rather it is a noble profession of serving the country and the globe with thriving human resources as an asset, instead of family and social burden.

Our partnership model is long-term, aligning with our vision, mission and objectives. We are working as a dedicated mason to build our nation educated.

Our Vision

To build a solid business domain from where we can provide the best Japanese & English language training and visa support across the country — and contribute to the betterment of Bangladesh.

Our Mission

To provide the best Japanese & English language training along with the best visa processing service to people of Bangladesh, irrespective of where they live or what is their academic background — through unparalleled service, professional advice and innovative communication.

Strength & Collaboration

Built for a Globally Connected World

EETC operates in a highly competitive market and ensures implementation of service standards according to global demand. As we live in a global village where information technology has reached an excel level, higher study abroad has become a common phenomenon for both elite and middle class people in Bangladesh.

Japan and the UK have opened their immigration doors to welcome professionals and international students to minimize the labor gap. Bangladesh can grab this opportunity for mutual long-term benefits — Japan & UK need working-aged people and Bangladesh needs a knowledge-based population.
