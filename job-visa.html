Japan's fast-track work visa for skilled workers in 14 industries. Lower entry barrier, real career opportunity.

What is SSW?

Specified Skilled Worker Visa

Introduced by the Japanese government in 2019, the SSW visa addresses Japan's labor shortage in 14 specific industries. Unlike the Engineer visa, SSW does not require a university degree — making it ideal for skilled workers with vocational training or experience.

Requirements

How to Qualify for SSW

1. Japanese Language Test

Pass JFT-Basic (A2 level) OR JLPT N4 minimum. Care-worker sector also accepts the Japanese Nursing Care Test.

2. Sector Skill Test

Pass the industry-specific skill test for your chosen sector. Tests are conducted in Bangladesh and several other countries.

3. Employer Match

Receive a job offer from a registered Japanese employer. EETC connects you with verified employers.

Benefits

Why SSW Visa is the Smart Choice

No Degree Needed

Vocational training and experience are sufficient.

Equal Pay

Same salary as Japanese workers in the same role.

5-Year Initial

SSW (i) lasts 5 years; SSW (ii) renewable indefinitely.

Family Visa (SSW ii)

Bring your spouse and children under SSW (ii).

For Entrepreneurs

Business Visa

経営・管理ビザ — Business Manager Visa

For entrepreneurs and investors setting up or managing a business in Japan.

Overview

Run Your Business in Japan

The Business Manager Visa (経営・管理) lets foreign nationals start or manage a company in Japan. With Japan's stable economy, world-class infrastructure and growing openness to foreign investment, it's an excellent gateway for entrepreneurs from Bangladesh.

EETC partners with licensed administrative scriveners (行政書士) and tax consultants for end-to-end company setup.

Requirements

Key Requirements

¥5M Capital

Minimum capital investment of ¥5,000,000 in the Japan-registered company.

Physical Office

A real, leased office address in Japan — virtual offices generally rejected.

Employees

2 full-time Japanese-resident employees OR ¥5M capital alternative.

Business Plan

Detailed plan with revenue forecasts and operational scope.

Process

Setup & Visa Process

1. Initial Consultation

Discuss business idea, capital, type of company (KK / GK).

2. Office Lease

Secure a registered office address in Japan.

3. Company Registration

Articles of Incorporation, capital deposit, registration with Legal Affairs Bureau.

4. Tax & Bank Setup

Tax office notification, social insurance and corporate bank account.

5. COE Application

Certificate of Eligibility filed with Japanese immigration.

6. Visa Stamping

Visa stamp at Japanese embassy in Dhaka — 1 year initial, then renewable.
